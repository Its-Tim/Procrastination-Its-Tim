Procrastination: How to Move Forward

What is Procrastination?

Procrastination is postponing or delaying tasks, often due to distractions or difficulties with time management. Procrastination often leads to stress, and a decrease in productivity. It is a very common behavior that can have serious consequences if not managed effectively.

Why is it Important to Overcome Procrastination?

Overcoming procrastination is major for reducing stess and improving productivity. According to a 2014 study on procrastination, procrastination is connected to negative functioning and risks to mental health.

How to Prioritize Tasks to help Overcome Procrastination

How to Prioritize Tasks

Image by Mohamed Hassan from Pixabay

  • Set goals like completing tasks a day ahead of time, or breaking larger tasks into smaller ones.
  • Make a to-do list, you should plan things ahead of time.
  • Avoid Multitasking, you should work on one task at a time to maintain focus.
  • Focus on urgent and important tasks first.

Tips to Overcome Procrastination

How to Overcome Procrastination
  • Eliminate distractions... specifically social media, video games and television.
  • Take small breaks between tasks. Doing this can help you to reduce stress and helps you to relax your mind.
  • Motivate yourself by giving yourself rewards after completing a task.
  • Set deadines by aiming to finish tasks ahead of time to reduce last-minute pressure. This can help reduce stress levels aswell.

How Distractions can cause Procrastination

Distractions delay attention from doing the main tasks, which then leads to poor efficiency and performance.

Removing the distractions clears the way for focus, which leads to you performing your work better.

Because of distractions, trying to get back into the groove of working affects the quality of work and increases the time spent on tasks.
